What is Scatter

Scatter is a Decentralized-Application Bridge for the EOS Blockchain.

If a decentralized application is the bridge between you and the blockchain, then Scatter is the encrypted bridge between you and the application. It handles your keys, accounts, and various other user-set definitions such as identities and private data.

Take a shopping website as an example. Normally you have to click buy, then enter your private keys, shipping address, email, and any other information they require in order to ship your purchase to you, then you have to create an account to track your order which again requires your email and a password. With Scatter you simply click buy, accept the transaction with the account of your choosing, and you’re done.


Why use Scatter

Copy pasting your confidential information such as keys leaves you open to various vulnerabilities. It’s fairly easy to mispell something and end up sending your money to an unintended account or have your personal information stolen by man-in-the-middle attacks, keyloggers, or even just some guy accross the street with a pair of binoculars.

Let’s look at some of the various ways that Scatter helps secure your accounts, transactions, keys and money.

Identities can be configured on Scatter and requested by applications without you having to fill out the same form a thousand times on different applications. They can also hold locational data such as addresses and personal data such as phone numbers, real names and email addresses. When a website wants to register you as a User, they simply request an identity and you never have to enter another password again since they can verify you using proven cryptographic technologies.

You can set up multiple identities and select which one to use at will. This is great for when you want different levels of anonymity for different websites, or simply different shipping addresses for different locations.
